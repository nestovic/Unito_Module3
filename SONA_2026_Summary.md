# Summary of President Cyril Ramaphosa's 2026 State of the Nation Address (SONA)

Delivered on February 12, 2026, the address centers on South Africa reaching a "turning point" from an era of decline toward one of prosperity and growth.

## Key Themes and Initiatives

### 1. Infrastructure and Economic Growth
*   **Massive Investment:** A record **R1 trillion** public investment committed over three years to build and maintain infrastructure.
*   **Operation Vulindlela:** Continued focus on transforming the structure of the economy to make electricity, water, and logistics sectors more efficient.
*   **Specialized Courts:** Establishment of specialized commercial courts to fast-track legal matters affecting the economy and development.

### 2. Energy Security
*   **Ending Loadshedding:** The President declared that "loadshedding is behind us," shifting focus to long-term energy security.
*   **Renewable Transition:** Aiming for more than **40% renewable energy** supply by 2030.
*   **Eskom Restructuring:** Establishing a fully independent state-owned transmission entity to create a level playing field for competition.

### 3. Water and Local Government Reform
*   **National Water Crisis Committee:** A new coordinating body chaired by the President to address water outages.
*   **Accountability:** Criminal charges have been laid against 56 municipalities; the state will now move to charge Municipal Managers in their personal capacities for violations.
*   **Incentives:** A new **R54 billion incentive** for metros to reform water, sanitation, and electricity services.

### 4. Digital Transformation
*   **Digital ID:** The Department of Home Affairs will launch a Digital ID this year.
*   **MyMzansi Platform:** A new platform to digitize driver's licenses, Matric certificates, and police statements, reducing the need for manual forms and office visits.

### 5. Agriculture and Crisis Management
*   **Foot-and-Mouth Disease:** Classified as a national disaster. The state will facilitate the vaccination of the national herd (14 million cattle) with 28 million vaccines over the next 12 months.
*   **Support:** Deployment of 10,000 new Extension Officers to support farmers and improve productivity.

### 6. International Relations and Sovereignty
*   **G20 Presidency:** Building on the success of South Africa’s G20 Presidency to advance the priorities of the Global South.
*   **Global Solidarity:** Continued support for international law and human rights, specifically mentioning Palestine, Cuba, Sudan, and Western Sahara.
*   **Peacekeeping:** Consolidating the Defence Force by withdrawing troops from the UN Mission in the DRC.

### 7. National Dialogue
*   **Post-2030 Vision:** The National Dialogue process will culminate in a National Convention to guide the next phase of the National Development Plan beyond 2030.

## Conclusion
The President called for 2026 to be a year of "transformative change," drawing inspiration from the 70th anniversary of the 1956 Women's March to fix local government, fight crime, and build a state that works for all citizens.
